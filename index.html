About

I'm Rishit — a 16-year-old builder from Singapore.

I study Commerce and Applied Mathematics at GIIS Singapore, but most of what I care about happens outside the classroom. I've built and shipped three products, one of which is still active. I think seriously about markets, specifically the ones that conventional wisdom writes off as too small or too weird — like the teen economy.

Teenagers are active economic participants. They buy, sell, trade, and transact constantly — and almost no serious infrastructure has been built for them. That gap is where I spend most of my thinking time.

Outside of building: I play competitive chess (FIDE 1573), read about market microstructure, and moderate communities. I like problems that look simple until you get close enough to see all the ways they aren't.
